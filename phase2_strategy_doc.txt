## **FOUNDATION: Why This Strategy Exists**

### **The Institutional Behavior We Exploit**

**Large institutions (pension funds, mutual funds, hedge funds) execute orders using VWAP algorithms:**

- When they need to buy 500,000 shares of AAPL, they can't just buy all at once (would move the market)
- Instead, they use **VWAP algorithms** that try to buy at the average price throughout the day
- These algorithms have **programmatic buy/sell rules** tied to VWAP:
  - **When price < VWAP** → Algorithm BUYS (trying to get below-average prices)
  - **When price > VWAP** → Algorithm SELLS (taking profits above average)

**This creates predictable oscillation patterns:**
- Price drifts below VWAP → Institutions buy → Price recovers toward VWAP
- Price overshoots above VWAP → Institutions sell → Price falls back toward VWAP
- VWAP acts like a **magnet** - price constantly pulled back toward it

**Our Edge:**
We identify when this institutional buying pressure is about to kick in (the recovery pattern), enter just as it starts, and ride the wave back to VWAP and beyond.

## **THE 4-STEP VWAP RECOVERY PATTERN**

This is the **entry signal** - we only trade when we see this exact sequence:

---

### **STEP 1: DECLINE (Initial Deviation)**

**What Happens:**
- Price drops **~1% from recent high** (configurable: 0.5% to 2%)
- This creates separation from VWAP (price now trading below average)

**Example:**
- Recent high: $100.00
- Price drops to: $99.00 (1% decline)
- VWAP at this moment: ~$99.50 (price is below VWAP)

**Why This Matters:**
- Creates the "deviation" that institutional algorithms will want to correct
- Signals temporary selling pressure or profit-taking
- Sets up the rebound potential

**What We're Watching:**
- Magnitude of decline (need at least 1%)
- Speed of decline (gradual is better than sudden crash)
- Volume during decline (normal volume preferred, not panic selling)

---

### **STEP 2: 50% RECOVERY (Rebound Begins)**

**What Happens:**
- Price rebounds by **exactly 50% of that decline**
- Shows buyers stepping in, but not full reversal yet

**Example:**
- Started at: $100.00
- Dropped to: $99.00 (1% decline = $1.00 drop)
- Rebounds to: $99.50 (50% of $1.00 = $0.50 recovery)

**Why This Matters:**
- Confirms buyers are interested (not dead cat bounce)
- Shows institutional algos starting to accumulate
- 50% recovery is statistically significant threshold (not random noise)

**What We're Watching:**
- Clean rebound (not choppy or erratic)
- Volume during rebound (should increase from decline volume)
- Time to achieve 50% (faster = stronger conviction)

---

### **STEP 3: 50% RETRACEMENT (The "Last Low")**

**What Happens:**
- Price **pulls back 50% of the recovery**
- This establishes our critical "last low" reference point
- Creates a pause/consolidation before final leg up

**Example:**
- Rebounded to: $99.50
- Recovery amount: $0.50
- Retraces 50%: Falls back to $99.25 (50% of $0.50 = $0.25 pullback)
- **This $99.25 becomes our "last low"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Healthy patterns retrace before continuing (tests support)
- Shakes out weak hands (people who bought the initial bounce)
- Stronger buyers accumulate at this dip
- Creates a clear reference point for entry confirmation

**What We're Watching:**
- Retracement magnitude (should be ~50%, not 100% or 10%)
- Volume during retracement (should be lower than rebound volume)
- Price holds above a certain level (doesn't break too far down)

---

### **STEP 4: CONFIRMATION (Entry Trigger)**

**What Happens:**
- Price **climbs 1.5% to 2.0% from the "last low"**
- This confirms buyers have regained control
- **ONLY NOW DO WE ENTER**

**Example:**
- Last low: $99.25
- Confirmation threshold: 1.5% climb = $99.25 × 1.015 = $100.74
- **When price hits $100.74 → WE BUY**

**Why This Matters:**
- Proves the pattern is real (not false signal)
- Confirms institutional buying is active
- Reduces false entries (most failed patterns don't confirm)
- Entry price is above VWAP (riding momentum, not catching falling knife)

**What We're Watching:**
- Speed of climb (faster = stronger)
- Volume during confirmation (should be highest of entire pattern)
- VWAP position (ideally price crosses above VWAP during confirmation)
- Time elapsed (confirmation should happen within 3-8 minutes)

## **EXIT STRATEGY: X THEN X+ LOGIC**

This is the **critical innovation** - we have dual profit targets with intelligent fallback.

---

### **SCENARIO A: Hit Target 1 First** (Most Common - ~60% of winning trades)

**What Happens:**
1. Price rises to Target 1 (+0.75%)
2. **DO NOT SELL** - Mark as "Target 1 achieved"
3. **Now monitor for two outcomes:**

**Outcome A1: Continues to Target 2**
- Price keeps climbing to +2.0%
- **SELL ALL at Target 2**
- **Result: +2.0% profit**
- Example: Entry $100.74 → Exit $102.75 = $55.00 profit on $3,000 position

**Outcome A2: Falls Back to Target 1**
- Price doesn't reach Target 2
- Price falls back down to Target 1 level (+0.75%)
- **SELL ALL at Target 1**
- **Result: +0.75% profit**
- Example: Entry $100.74 → Exit $101.50 = $22.50 profit on $3,000 position

**Why This Works:**
- Gives upside a chance (don't cut winners short)
- Protects profits if momentum fades (guaranteed +0.75% minimum)
- Captures the "extension" when institutional buying is strong
- Average win: ~1.4% (weighted between 0.75% and 2.0%)

---

### **SCENARIO B: Hit Stop Loss** (~35% of trades)

**What Happens:**
1. Price falls to -0.5% from entry
2. **SELL IMMEDIATELY** (no waiting)
3. **Result: -0.5% loss**
- Example: Entry $100.74 → Exit $100.24 = -$15.00 loss on $3,000 position

**Why We Accept This:**
- Small, controlled losses are part of the strategy
- Prevents larger drawdowns
- Win rate is 65%, so we win more than we lose
- Average loss is smaller than average win (1.4% vs 0.5%)

---

### **SCENARIO C: Pattern Fails Before Entry** (~25% of detected patterns)

**What Happens:**
1. Steps 1-3 complete (decline, recovery, retracement)
2. Price **never climbs the 1.5% confirmation threshold**
3. Pattern dissipates or reverses
4. **NO TRADE EXECUTED**
5. **Result: Zero capital risked**

**Why This Is Important:**
- Most patterns fail before confirmation
- We don't enter failed patterns (unlike many strategies)
- Confirmation filter eliminates ~25% of false signals
- Only trade high-probability setups

### **Account-Level Risk Controls:**

**Daily Loss Limit (Circuit Breaker):**
- If account realizes **-1.5% loss in a single day**:
  - railyard.py **STOPS all new entries**
  - Open positions still managed (can hit targets or stops)
  - System logs event to database
  - Email/SMS alerts to co-founders
  - **Resets next trading day at 9:31 AM**

**Example:**
- Start of day: $30,000
- Loss limit: $30,000 × -1.5% = -$450
- If account hits $29,550 → circuit breaker trips

**Capital Deployment:**
- **Months 1-6:** 80% deployed, 20% reserve
- **Month 7+:** 50% deployed, 50% reserve (taper for protection)
- Reserve ensures buying power for opportunities
- Prevents full capital at risk

**Spread & Liquidity Guards:**
- **Max spread:** 2.0 bps to enter
- **Min NBBO depth:** 2,000 shares
- If spread too wide → skip entry
- If liquidity too thin → skip entry

**Halt Detection:**
- If no ticks received for 10 seconds → assume halt
- Pause all new entries
- Monitor existing positions
- Resume when ticks flow again

**Latency Guardian:**
- If order routing latency exceeds 50ms:
  - Pause new entries until stable
  - Prevent slippage from slow execution
  - Resume when latency <50ms consistently

```
E = (Win_Rate × Avg_Win) - (Loss_Rate × Avg_Loss) - Friction

Where:
Win_Rate = 65% (historical backtest)
Avg_Win = 1.4% (weighted: 60% hit T1 @ 0.75%, 40% hit T2 @ 2.0%)
Loss_Rate = 35%
Avg_Loss = 0.5%
Friction = 0.04% (spreads + commissions)

Calculation:
E = (0.65 × 1.4%) - (0.35 × 0.5%) - 0.04%
E = 0.91% - 0.175% - 0.04%
E ≈ 0.695% per trade
```

## **DEAD ZONE DETECTION & MANAGEMENT**

### **What is a Dead Zone?**

**Definition:**
A trade that enters a low-volatility, sideways range where:
- Position is open for 8+ minutes
- Price range is <0.6% (stuck in tight range)
- Neither hitting targets nor stop loss
- "Zombie trade" - capital tied up, no progress

**Example:**
- Entry: $100.74
- After 10 minutes: $100.80 (barely moved)
- P&L: +0.06% (nowhere near +0.75% target)
- **This is a dead zone**

---

### **Why Dead Zones Matter:**

**Opportunity Cost:**
- While stuck in TSLA dead zone, NVDA has 2 new patterns
- Capital is tied up, can't enter better setups
- Missing profitable opportunities elsewhere

**Psychological Drain:**
- Watching paint dry
- Temptation to exit manually (bad discipline)
- Reduces confidence in strategy

**Performance Drag:**
- Average trade time should be 5-15 minutes
- Dead zones extend to 30-60 minutes
- Reduces daily trade capacity

---

### **Dead Zone Analysis During Morning Report:**

**What We Track (from 20-day historical analysis):**

1. **Dead Zone Frequency:**
   - How often does this stock produce dead zones?
   - Example: AAPL = 15% of trades, TSLA = 8%

2. **Dead Zone Duration:**
   - How long do dead zones last?
   - Example: AAPL avg = 18 minutes, NVDA avg = 12 minutes

3. **Dead Zone Opportunity Cost:**
   - Missed patterns × Avg Win × Position Size
   - Example: 2 missed patterns × 1.4% × $3,000 = $84 lost

4. **Dead Zone Recovery Rate:**
   - What % of dead zones eventually become profitable?
   - Example: 40% still hit targets (just took longer)

5. **Time-of-Day Heat Map:**
   - When do dead zones occur?
   - Example: AAPL dead zones spike 11:00 AM - 12:00 PM (lunch lull)

**Dead Zone Severity Score (0-100):**
```
Severity = (Frequency × 40) + (Avg_Duration/30 × 30) + (Opp_Cost/200 × 30)

Interpretation:
0-20: Excellent (low dead zone risk)
21-35: Good
36-50: Fair
51-70: Poor (consider excluding stock)
71-100: Avoid (chronic dead zones)
```

---

### **Dead Zone Handling in Live Trading:**

**Detection:**
- If position open >8 minutes
- AND price range <0.6%
- AND not approaching targets/stop
- **Flag as dead zone**

**Action (Current):**
- Continue holding (targets/stop still active)
- Log event to database
- Track opportunity cost

**Future Enhancement:**
- Time-weighted exits (exit after 15 min if in dead zone)
- Dead zone-aware position sizing (smaller size for high-risk stocks)
- Avoid entry during high-risk time windows
