## **FOUNDATION: Why This Strategy Exists**

### **The Market Behavior We Exploit**

**Large crypto traders and market makers execute orders using VWAP algorithms:**

- When they need to buy $5M of BTC, they can't just buy all at once (would move the market)
- Instead, they use **VWAP algorithms** that try to buy at the average price throughout the day
- These algorithms have **programmatic buy/sell rules** tied to VWAP:
  - **When price < VWAP** → Algorithm BUYS (trying to get below-average prices)
  - **When price > VWAP** → Algorithm SELLS (taking profits above average)

**This creates predictable oscillation patterns:**
- Price drifts below VWAP → Market makers buy → Price recovers toward VWAP
- Price overshoots above VWAP → Market makers sell → Price falls back toward VWAP
- VWAP acts like a **magnet** - price constantly pulled back toward it

**Our Edge:**
We identify when this buying pressure is about to kick in (the recovery pattern), enter just as it starts, and ride the wave back to VWAP and beyond.

**Platform:** Polymarket US when crypto markets launch. We trade crypto directly on the Polymarket CLOB — same infrastructure, same SDK, same WebSocket feeds as our Phase 1 sports trading. No third-party price comparison. Pure pattern-based trading.

## **THE 4-STEP VWAP RECOVERY PATTERN**

This is the **entry signal** - we only trade when we see this exact sequence:

---

### **STEP 1: DECLINE (Initial Deviation)**

**What Happens:**
- Price drops **~1% from recent high** (configurable: 0.5% to 2%)
- This creates separation from VWAP (price now trading below average)

**Example (BTC 5-minute market):**
- Recent high: $68,000
- Price drops to: $67,320 (1% decline)
- VWAP at this moment: ~$67,660 (price is below VWAP)

**Why This Matters:**
- Creates the "deviation" that market maker algorithms will want to correct
- Signals temporary selling pressure or profit-taking
- Sets up the rebound potential

**What We're Watching:**
- Magnitude of decline (need at least 1%)
- Speed of decline (gradual is better than sudden crash)
- Volume during decline (normal volume preferred, not panic selling)

---

### **STEP 2: 50% RECOVERY (Rebound Begins)**

**What Happens:**
- Price rebounds by **exactly 50% of that decline**
- Shows buyers stepping in, but not full reversal yet

**Example:**
- Started at: $68,000
- Dropped to: $67,320 (1% decline = $680 drop)
- Rebounds to: $67,660 (50% of $680 = $340 recovery)

**Why This Matters:**
- Confirms buyers are interested (not dead cat bounce)
- Shows market maker algos starting to accumulate
- 50% recovery is statistically significant threshold (not random noise)

**What We're Watching:**
- Clean rebound (not choppy or erratic)
- Volume during rebound (should increase from decline volume)
- Time to achieve 50% (faster = stronger conviction)

---

### **STEP 3: 50% RETRACEMENT (The "Last Low")**

**What Happens:**
- Price **pulls back 50% of the recovery**
- This establishes our critical "last low" reference point
- Creates a pause/consolidation before final leg up

**Example:**
- Rebounded to: $67,660
- Recovery amount: $340
- Retraces 50%: Falls back to $67,490 (50% of $340 = $170 pullback)
- **This $67,490 becomes our "last low"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Healthy patterns retrace before continuing (tests support)
- Shakes out weak hands (people who bought the initial bounce)
- Stronger buyers accumulate at this dip
- Creates a clear reference point for entry confirmation

**What We're Watching:**
- Retracement magnitude (should be ~50%, not 100% or 10%)
- Volume during retracement (should be lower than rebound volume)
- Price holds above a certain level (doesn't break too far down)

---

### **STEP 4: CONFIRMATION (Entry Trigger)**

**What Happens:**
- Price **climbs 1.5% to 2.0% from the "last low"**
- This confirms buyers have regained control
- **ONLY NOW DO WE ENTER**

**Example:**
- Last low: $67,490
- Confirmation threshold: 1.5% climb = $67,490 × 1.015 = $68,502
- **When price hits $68,502 → WE BUY**

**Why This Matters:**
- Proves the pattern is real (not false signal)
- Confirms market maker buying is active
- Reduces false entries (most failed patterns don't confirm)
- Entry price is above VWAP (riding momentum, not catching falling knife)

**What We're Watching:**
- Speed of climb (faster = stronger)
- Volume during confirmation (should be highest of entire pattern)
- VWAP position (ideally price crosses above VWAP during confirmation)
- Time elapsed (confirmation should happen within 3-8 minutes)

## **EXIT STRATEGY: X THEN X+ LOGIC**

This is the **critical innovation** - we have dual profit targets with intelligent fallback.

---

### **SCENARIO A: Hit Target 1 First** (Most Common - ~60% of winning trades)

**What Happens:**
1. Price rises to Target 1 (+0.75%)
2. **DO NOT SELL** - Mark as "Target 1 achieved"
3. **Now monitor for two outcomes:**

**Outcome A1: Continues to Target 2**
- Price keeps climbing to +2.0%
- **SELL ALL at Target 2**
- **Result: +2.0% profit**

**Outcome A2: Falls Back to Target 1**
- Price doesn't reach Target 2
- Price falls back down to Target 1 level (+0.75%)
- **SELL ALL at Target 1**
- **Result: +0.75% profit**

**Why This Works:**
- Gives upside a chance (don't cut winners short)
- Protects profits if momentum fades (guaranteed +0.75% minimum)
- Captures the "extension" when buying pressure is strong
- Average win: ~1.4% (weighted between 0.75% and 2.0%)

---

### **SCENARIO B: Hit Stop Loss** (~35% of trades)

**What Happens:**
1. Price falls to -0.5% from entry
2. **SELL IMMEDIATELY** (no waiting)
3. **Result: -0.5% loss**

**Why We Accept This:**
- Small, controlled losses are part of the strategy
- Prevents larger drawdowns
- Win rate is 65%, so we win more than we lose
- Average loss is smaller than average win (1.4% vs 0.5%)

---

### **SCENARIO C: Pattern Fails Before Entry** (~25% of detected patterns)

**What Happens:**
1. Steps 1-3 complete (decline, recovery, retracement)
2. Price **never climbs the 1.5% confirmation threshold**
3. Pattern dissipates or reverses
4. **NO TRADE EXECUTED**
5. **Result: Zero capital risked**

## **DEAD ZONE DETECTION & MANAGEMENT**

### **What is a Dead Zone?**

**Definition:**
A trade that enters a low-volatility, sideways range where:
- Position is open for 8+ minutes
- Price range is <0.6% (stuck in tight range)
- Neither hitting targets nor stop loss
- Capital tied up with no progress

**Detection:**
- If position open >8 minutes
- AND price range <0.6%
- AND not approaching targets/stop
- **Flag as dead zone**

**Action:**
- Continue holding (targets/stop still active)
- Log event to database
- Track opportunity cost
- Time-weighted exit after 15 minutes if still in dead zone
